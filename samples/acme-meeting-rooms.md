# Acme Inc. — Meeting Room Booking

*For internal use. Last updated: 2026-02-28.*

Acme operates two physical hubs. Each hub has a small number of bookable rooms named after capital cities. This page tells you how to find, book, and use them.

## How to Book a Room

1. Open your work calendar (Google Workspace).
2. Create the meeting as you normally would.
3. Click **Add rooms** in the location picker and start typing the room name (e.g. `Helsinki`).
4. Pick the one you want. If it shows in red, it is double-booked — try another, or use the suggested-time feature.

Rooms accept bookings up to 8 weeks in advance. Recurring bookings are limited to 3 months at a time, to keep schedules fresh.

## Booking Rules

- **No-show window**: 10 minutes. If you do not check in by tapping the room display, the booking is released and anyone can claim the room.
- **Maximum length**: 4 hours per booking. Book in chunks if you need longer (gives others a chance to grab a slot).
- **Lunch hours**: 12:00 – 13:00 local time is reserved for ad-hoc bookings; you cannot pre-book it for a regular meeting.
- **External visitors**: Allowed. Sign visitors in at reception before the meeting.
- **Off-hours**: Rooms can be used outside business hours but must be re-set (chairs, whiteboards) before you leave.

## Equipment

- **Hybrid meetings**: The conference camera and microphone automatically connect to the room display when a call starts. If they do not, restart the panel (the small touchscreen on the table).
- **Whiteboards**: Use only dry-erase markers. Erase the board at the end of your meeting unless you have written `KEEP UNTIL <date>` clearly at the top.
- **Cables**: HDMI and USB-C cables live in the table drawer. If they are missing, IT will replace them — open a ticket in `#it-help`.
- **Adapters**: There is a basket of USB-C / USB-A / VGA / DisplayPort adapters at each hub reception.

## Issues

| Problem | Who to ask |
|---|---|
| Display won't turn on | IT (`#it-help`) |
| Room is double-booked | Whoever booked first wins; loser asks Reception for an alternative |
| Visitor cannot get past reception | Reception, then the meeting host |
| Whiteboard markers dried out | Reception drawer |
| AC too cold / too warm | Office Operations (`#office-ops`) |

## Quiet Spaces

Both hubs have unbookable quiet rooms intended for focused work:

- Berlin Hub: First-floor north corner.
- Helsinki Hub: Second floor, behind the kitchen.

Quiet rooms enforce a no-talking, no-call rule. Please use a phone booth for calls.

## Visitor Policy Summary

- Visitors must be pre-registered at least 2 hours in advance via `https://intranet.example.com/visitors`.
- They sign in at reception, receive a visitor badge, and must be escorted by their host at all times.
- Walk-in visitors (without pre-registration) are turned away by reception. No exceptions, including senior leadership.
