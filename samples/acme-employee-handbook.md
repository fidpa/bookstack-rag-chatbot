# Acme Inc. Employee Handbook

*Last updated: 2026-01-15*

Welcome to Acme Inc. This handbook collects the policies that apply to everyone on the team. It is the source of truth — if something here contradicts what a colleague tells you, this document wins. If something here is unclear, ask the People team in `#people-help` on Slack.

## Working Hours

Acme runs a flexible-hours model with a small mandatory overlap window.

- **Core hours**: 10:00 to 15:00 local time. You are expected to be reachable during these hours.
- **Total weekly hours**: 38 hours per week for full-time staff. 4-day weeks are allowed by agreement with your manager and the People team.
- **Time tracking**: Salaried employees do not track hours. Hourly contractors record time in Harvest.
- **Public holidays**: Observed according to the calendar of the country in which you are employed. See `acme-vacation-policy.md` for the full list.

## Compensation and Benefits

- **Salary review**: Once a year, in March. Off-cycle adjustments are possible for promotions or scope changes.
- **Bonus**: Discretionary annual bonus, paid in April for the previous calendar year. Bonus targets are 10 % of base salary for non-managers and 15 % for managers.
- **Equity**: Stock options granted at start, vesting over four years with a one-year cliff.
- **Health insurance**: Acme covers 100 % of the employee premium and 80 % of dependent premiums.
- **Pension / 401(k)**: 5 % employer match on contributions up to 5 % of salary.
- **Learning budget**: USD 2 000 per person per year for books, courses, and conferences. Submit receipts to `learning@example.com`.
- **Equipment budget**: USD 3 000 every three years for laptop, monitor, and peripherals.

## Remote and Hybrid Work

- **Remote first**: Acme is a remote-first company. Most teams have no offices.
- **Hubs**: We maintain co-working hubs in Berlin and Helsinki. Hub access is by reservation through the People team.
- **Travel**: Two team off-sites per year, fully paid by Acme. One company-wide all-hands per year.
- **Time zones**: Teams are expected to have at least three hours of overlap in their working day. New hires choose a time zone band at signing.

## Communication

- **Slack**: Primary asynchronous communication. Default to public channels.
- **Email**: Used for external communication only.
- **Meetings**: Capped at 25 or 50 minutes (not 30 or 60) to leave breaks. No-meeting Wednesdays apply company-wide.
- **Documentation**: All decisions live in BookStack. Slack threads are not considered durable records.

## IT and Security Basics

The full IT onboarding flow is documented separately in `acme-it-onboarding.md`. Quick rules:

- Enable full-disk encryption on every device.
- Use the company password manager (1Password). Never reuse passwords across services.
- Two-factor authentication is mandatory on every Acme system. Authenticator apps are preferred; SMS is not allowed.
- Report any phishing attempt or suspicious access to `security@example.com` within 24 hours.

## Performance and Feedback

- Two formal reviews per year, in March and September.
- Continuous feedback is expected. If you have a concern, raise it within two weeks of the triggering event.
- Promotion decisions are made by a calibration committee, not by individual managers.

## Leaving Acme

We try to keep offboarding humane. When you decide to leave:

1. Give notice in writing to your manager and to People.
2. Notice period is 4 weeks for non-managers and 8 weeks for managers, unless a different period is in your contract.
3. Hand over open work, including in-flight decisions and documents.
4. Return company equipment in the prepaid return packaging the IT team will send you.
5. Final paycheck includes any unused vacation days, capped at 10 days carry-over.

## Questions

If anything in this handbook is unclear, contradicts itself, or feels outdated, please open a Pull Request against the wiki repository or ask in `#people-help`.
