# Acme Inc. Vacation and Leave Policy

*Last updated: 2026-01-22*

Acme believes that rested people make better decisions. This policy sets out how much time off you get, how to request it, and what happens to unused days.

## Annual Vacation Allowance

| Role band | Days per calendar year |
|---|---|
| Full-time, year 1 | 25 days |
| Full-time, year 2–4 | 28 days |
| Full-time, year 5+ | 30 days |
| Part-time (4 days/week) | Pro-rata, rounded up to the nearest whole day |
| Contractors | As per individual contract |

These are calendar days off, not workdays. A typical 5-day workweek therefore uses 5 vacation days, not 7.

## Public Holidays

Public holidays are observed in your country of employment and do **not** count against your vacation allowance. For example, an employee based in Germany observes German public holidays; an employee based in Finland observes Finnish ones.

If a public holiday falls on a weekend, there is no in-lieu day off — this matches local labour law in most of the countries we operate in.

## Requesting Vacation

1. Submit a request in our HR system at least **two weeks** in advance for vacations of one week or longer, and **two working days** in advance for shorter ones.
2. Your manager approves or declines within 48 hours.
3. If your manager declines, they must propose two alternative date ranges within 30 days.

We strongly encourage taking at least one continuous two-week vacation per year.

## Sick Leave

- **Short-term sickness** (up to three working days): Notify your manager in the morning of the first day; no doctor's note needed.
- **Longer sickness** (four or more working days): A doctor's certificate is required from day four onwards, sent to `people@example.com`.
- **Long-term sickness** (more than two weeks): The People team will reach out to discuss support and statutory pay where applicable.

Sick leave does not count against vacation allowance.

## Parental Leave

- **Primary caregiver**: 16 weeks fully paid, on top of any statutory entitlement. Can be taken in up to three blocks within 12 months of the child's arrival.
- **Secondary caregiver**: 6 weeks fully paid, on top of statutory entitlement.
- **Adoption and surrogacy**: Treated identically to birth.
- **Returning**: Phased return option (4-day weeks for the first 8 weeks) on request.

## Bereavement Leave

- 5 working days paid for immediate family (spouse, partner, parent, child, sibling).
- 2 working days paid for extended family (grandparent, in-law).
- Additional unpaid leave by agreement with your manager.

## Carry-over Rules

- Unused vacation can be carried over to the next year, up to a **maximum of 10 days**.
- Carry-over must be used by 31 March of the following year, or it is forfeited.
- When you leave Acme, any unused days within the carry-over cap are paid out.

## FAQ

**Q: I'm based in country X. Which public holidays apply?**
A: Whichever your local statutory list says. If you're not sure, ask `people@example.com`.

**Q: Can I take unpaid leave?**
A: Yes, by agreement with your manager and the People team. Up to 4 weeks per year without affecting tenure-based benefits.

**Q: Does vacation accrue continuously or by year?**
A: By calendar year. New joiners get a pro-rata allowance for their first year.

**Q: What about jury duty / military reserve duty?**
A: Paid leave for statutory civic duties up to 10 days per year. Bring the official summons / order.

**Q: Can I sell unused vacation days back?**
A: No. Acme does not buy back vacation days.
